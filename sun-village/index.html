📍 About the Community

Sun Village, Surprise AZ — the boutique, gated 55+ on Bell Road

Sun Village is the smallest and most intimate of the West Valley 55+ communities — just 1,382 homes inside a gated perimeter on Bell Road in Surprise. The community is part of "Retirement Row" — the same Bell Road corridor that runs through Sun City, Sun City West, Sun City Grand, Arizona Traditions, and ends at Sun City Festival in Buckeye.

Built in the mid-1980s, Sun Village offers low-maintenance patio homes and townhomes designed for compact, lock-and-leave retirement living. Single-family detached homes are available in the Pima, Hopi, Shawnee, Comanche, and Kiowa village neighborhoods. Patio homes cluster in Zuni Village, which has its own community center and pool in addition to the main amenities.

The centerpiece is a 47,000-square-foot community center with a ballroom, fitness rooms, full-service restaurant, billiards, and beauty salon. A par-54 18-hole walking golf course winds through the community, and the heated pool is one of the largest in Arizona at 180,000 gallons and over 7,000 sqft. Two pets per household.

❓ Real Questions Real Buyers Ask

Sun Village Buyer FAQ — Everything You Were Going to Google

Why is Sun Village so much smaller than the other Sun Cities?

Sun Village was developed as a boutique, intimate alternative to the giant master-planned 55+ communities. With 1,382 homes, it's about 1/20th the size of Sun City and 1/12th the size of Sun City West. The trade-off: fewer amenities and clubs, but a closer-knit neighborhood feel and a gated, more private setting.

What makes the golf course different at Sun Village?

Sun Village has a par-54 18-hole walking golf course — meaning all 18 holes are par-3s designed for walking (no cart required, though carts are allowed). This format is much easier on the body, faster to play, and ideal for retirees who want golf without the commitment of a full-length course.

Is Sun Village pet-friendly?

Yes — but with a 2-pets-per-household limit across all homes. This is stricter than most 55+ communities in the area. Dogs and cats are welcome; check the HOA rules for breed restrictions if you have a specific pet in mind.

What's the snowbird/lock-and-leave situation at Sun Village?

Sun Village is one of the most snowbird-friendly West Valley communities. The patio homes and townhomes are designed for lock-and-leave living, with HOA-maintained landscaping and exterior maintenance. The gated entry adds security for absentee owners. Many residents live here 6 months a year and rent or shutter the rest.
